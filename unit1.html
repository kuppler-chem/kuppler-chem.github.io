Unit Overview

In this 45-day unit, students explore the structure of matter, development of atomic theory, isotopes, electron configurations, periodic trends, and chemical bonding. Labs and a culminating project connect ideas to real-world applications.

Standards for Unit 1:  HS-PS1-1 HS-PS1-2 HS-PS1-8

Essential Questions

  1. EQ1: How does the structure of the atom determine the behavior of elements?
  2. EQ2: How do patterns in the periodic table help us predict properties and reactivity?

Week 1 (Days 1–5) — Matter & Atomic Theory Foundations

Focus Classification of matter, physical vs. chemical changes, early atomic models, isotopes.

Day 1 — Intro to Chemistry & Matter

Objective: Explain chemistry as the study of matter and its changes; understand course expectations.

In class: Syllabus overview; icebreaker; intro to matter (pure substances vs. mixtures).

Homework: Read textbook section on matter (pp. 2–10).

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — list two examples each of elements, compounds, and mixtures.

Day 2 — Classification of Matter

Objective: Classify matter into elements, compounds, and mixtures (homogeneous vs. heterogeneous).

In class: Sorting station activity with everyday items; quick-share.

Homework: Classification worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — categorize three given examples.

Day 3 — Physical vs. Chemical Properties

Objective: Distinguish between physical and chemical properties/changes.

In class: Demonstrations (ice melting, paper burning) with discussion; guided notes.

Homework: Questions 1–10 (p. 20).

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — identify physical vs. chemical change.

Day 4 — Early Atomic Models

Objective: Describe contributions of Dalton, Thomson, and Rutherford.

In class: Mini-lecture; gold foil experiment analysis.

Homework: Reading on atomic theory.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — match scientist to contribution.

Day 5 — Isotopes & Average Atomic Mass (Lab)

Objective: Define isotopes; calculate average atomic mass.

In class: Isotope “penny/bean” lab; class discussion.

Homework: Isotope practice sheet.

Checkpoint: Lab check — compute average atomic mass correctly.

Week 2 (Days 6–10) — Subatomic Structure & Electron Arrangement

Focus Subatomic particles, Bohr models, electron configurations, valence electrons, intro to periodic trends.

Day 6 — Atomic Structure (p+, n⁰, e⁻)

Objective: Interpret atomic structure using protons, neutrons, and electrons.

In class: Guided practice with nuclide symbols; whiteboard drills.

Homework: Subatomic counting practice.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — write isotope notation for oxygen.

Day 7 — Bohr Models

Objective: Construct Bohr models with energy levels.

In class: Mini-lesson; students draw Bohr models for selected elements.

Homework: Bohr model worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — Bohr model of sodium.

Day 8 — Electron Configurations (Aufbau)

Objective: Write electron configurations (up to Ca).

In class: Direct instruction on Aufbau; practice problems.

Homework: Electron configuration problem set.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — configuration for chlorine.

Day 9 — Valence Electrons

Objective: Identify valence electrons using the periodic table.

In class: Interactive periodic table highlight; practice predicting valence.

Homework: Valence electron worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — Mg and S valence electrons.

Day 10 — Periodic Trends Intro

Objective: Define/graph periodic trends (atomic radius, ionization energy).

In class: Guided notes; begin graphing activity in pairs.

Homework: Complete trend graphs.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — atomic radius trend across a period.

Week 3 (Days 11–15) — Periodicity & Bonding Basics

Focus Trend reasoning, flame test lab, bond types, bonding properties lab.

Day 11 — Periodic Trends in Depth

Objective: Explain trends using electron structure and shielding.

In class: Review graphs; discuss shielding & effective nuclear charge.

Homework: Practice questions on trends.

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — compare ionization energy (Na vs. Cl).

Day 12 — Flame Test Lab

Objective: Identify ions by flame color.

In class: Lab stations; record observations; class share-out.

Homework: Lab report write-up.

Checkpoint: Lab check — identify ≥3 ions correctly.

Day 13 — Bond Types

Objective: Differentiate ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding.

In class: Bond type mini-lesson; classification practice with examples.

Homework: Bonding worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — classify three compounds.

Day 14 — Bonding Properties Lab

Objective: Investigate melting point and conductivity for ionic vs covalent substances.

In class: Lab testing; compare results.

Homework: Lab report.

Checkpoint: Lab check — explain property differences by bond type.

Day 15 — Unit Review

Objective: Synthesize Unit 1 concepts.

In class: Review game; group problem set.

Homework: Study for quiz.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — strongest & weakest topic.

Week 4 (Days 16–20) — Assessment & Bonding Mastery

Focus Quiz, reteach, metallic bonding, bond comparisons, bonding quiz.

Day 16 — Unit Quiz

Objective: Assess mastery of concepts from Weeks 1–3.

In class: Unit quiz on atomic structure, isotopes, configurations, trends, bonding basics.

Homework: Reflection on missed items.

Checkpoint: Formal assessment — Quiz.

Day 17 — Quiz Review & Misconceptions

Objective: Clarify misconceptions and strengthen weak areas.

In class: Error analysis; small-group reteach rotations.

Homework: Differentiated practice packet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one clarified concept.

Day 18 — Metallic Bonding

Objective: Describe metallic bonding and its properties.

In class: “Sea of electrons” model; conductivity/malleability demos.

Homework: Metallic bonding worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — property explained by metallic bonding.

Day 19 — Bond Type Comparisons

Objective: Compare ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding through examples.

In class: Group comparison chart; case studies.

Homework: Mixed bond-type problem set.

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — classify substances.

Day 20 — Bonding Quiz

Objective: Demonstrate understanding of bonding.

In class: Quiz on ionic, covalent, metallic bonding.

Homework: Begin brainstorming project topics.

Checkpoint: Formal assessment — Quiz.

Week 5 (Days 21–25) — Project Introduction & Research Foundations

Focus Launch project, research skills, source evaluation, drafting, peer review.

Day 21 — Project Launch: Periodic Table Connection

Objective: Understand project goals, timeline, and rubric.

In class: Review guidelines; brainstorm real-world connections (materials, medicine, energy, environment).

Homework: Choose topic and bring 2 preliminary sources.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — submit proposed topic.

Day 22 — Research Workday

Objective: Gather credible sources and build an outline.

In class: Library/computer research; teacher conferences.

Homework: Continue research; complete outline draft.

Checkpoint: Outline due.

Day 23 — Evaluating Sources

Objective: Evaluate source credibility and relevance.

In class: Mini-lesson (CRAAP test); apply to own sources.

Homework: Annotated bibliography entries (2–3 sources).

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — reliable or not? (justify)

Day 24 — Drafting Content

Objective: Draft the first section of the project.

In class: Work session; teacher check-ins.

Homework: Finish section draft.

Checkpoint: Draft section submitted.

Day 25 — Peer Review

Objective: Improve clarity and accuracy through feedback.

In class: Peer review protocols; revision planning.

Homework: Apply revisions.

Checkpoint: Peer feedback submitted.

Week 6 (Days 26–30) — Project Refinement & Presentations

Focus Visuals, real-world applications, final draft, presentations.

Day 26 — Visuals & Applications

Objective: Create clear visuals and highlight real-world relevance.

In class: Work session; example critique (what makes a great figure?).

Homework: Finish visuals.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one real-world application described.

Day 27 — Draft Finalization

Objective: Finalize content for presentation.

In class: Writing polish; teacher conferencing.

Homework: Submit full draft.

Checkpoint: Draft submitted.

Day 28 — Presentations (Part 1)

Objective: Communicate findings clearly to peers.

In class: Student presentations; peer review notes.

Homework: Reflection on day’s talks.

Checkpoint: Presentation rubric scoring.

Day 29 — Presentations (Part 2)

Objective: Continue presentations with focus on evidence.

In class: Student presentations; Q&A.

Homework: Reflection questions.

Checkpoint: Presentation rubric scoring.

Day 30 — Final Presentations & Reflection

Objective: Conclude presentations; synthesize takeaways.

In class: Final talks; class discussion on themes.

Homework: Reflection essay on project.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one insight learned.

Week 7 (Days 31–35) — Reflection & Extension

Focus Reflection, comprehensive review, unit exam, corrections, enrichment.

Day 31 — Reflection on Projects

Objective: Reflect on outcomes and essential questions.

In class: Group discussion; reflection drafting.

Homework: Finish reflection draft.

Checkpoint: Reflection draft submitted.

Day 32 — Comprehensive Review

Objective: Prepare for Unit 1 exam.

In class: Jeopardy review; targeted problem sets; Q&A.

Homework: Study guide completion.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — two topics still unclear.

Day 33 — Unit 1 Exam

Objective: Demonstrate mastery of Unit 1 content.

In class: Unit exam.

Homework: None (exam day).

Checkpoint: Formal assessment — Exam.

Day 34 — Exam Debrief & Corrections

Objective: Analyze performance; correct misconceptions.

In class: Error analysis; small-group reteach; corrections.

Homework: Correction assignment.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one improved concept.

Day 35 — Enrichment Problems

Objective: Deepen understanding of trends and bonding through challenge problems.

In class: Enrichment sets; collaborative work & share-outs.

Homework: Finish enrichment problem of choice.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — solve one enrichment problem on board.

Week 8 (Days 36–40) — Case Studies & Applications

Focus Case studies, mole preview, multi-step challenges, synthesis, materials science.

Day 36 — Real-World Case Study

Objective: Apply Unit 1 concepts to real-world issues (e.g., isotopes in medicine or alloys in engineering).

In class: Case reading; small-group analysis; class debrief.

Homework: Short reflection linking case to Unit 1 ideas.

Checkpoint: Quick write — one connection explained.

Day 37 — Stoichiometry Preview (Mole Conversions)

Objective: Use mole-to-mass and particle conversions.

In class: Guided practice; conversion ladder strategies.

Homework: Mole conversion worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one mole-to-mass problem.

Day 38 — Challenge Problems

Objective: Solve multi-step challenges connecting atomic structure, trends, and bonding.

In class: Group problem rotations; quick presentations.

Homework: Complete one problem individually.

Checkpoint: Group solution presentation.

Day 39 — Synthesis & Discussion

Objective: Synthesize Unit 1 concepts; revisit essential questions.

In class: Whole-class discussion and quick-write synthesis.

Homework: Synthesis paragraph refinement.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — answer one essential question.

Day 40 — Applications of Bonding

Objective: Connect bonding to materials science (metals, ceramics, polymers).

In class: Mini-lesson; analyze examples.

Homework: Reflection on one material’s bonding.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — describe bonding in a chosen material.

Week 9 (Days 41–45) — Advanced Review & Transition to Unit 2

Focus Atomic theory applications, advanced configurations, final review, exam, transition to stoichiometry.

Day 41 — Atomic Theory Applications

Objective: Explore nuclear chemistry applications (e.g., imaging, decay).

In class: Short case; group Q&A.

Homework: Research a modern isotope application.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — summarize one application.

Day 42 — Advanced Electron Configurations

Objective: Practice configurations including transition metals.

In class: Spiral review; challenge items.

Homework: Configuration problem set.

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — write a configuration for a transition metal.

Day 43 — Final Review

Objective: Prepare for the comprehensive Unit 1 exam.

In class: Mixed-format review (MC/FRQ); peer teaching.

Homework: Study for the exam.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — identify last concept to review.

Day 44 — Comprehensive Unit 1 Exam

Objective: Demonstrate mastery across Unit 1.

In class: Unit 1 exam.

Homework: None (exam day).

Checkpoint: Formal assessment — Exam.

Day 45 — Reflection & Transition to Unit 2

Objective: Reflect on Unit 1 and preview stoichiometry.

In class: Exam debrief; reflection; Unit 2 preview.

Homework: Read Unit 2 introduction.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one way Unit 1 connects to stoichiometry.

End-of-Unit Project: Periodic Table Connection
Students research a real-world application of the periodic table (elements, families, or trends) and present findings with visuals and a reflection.
