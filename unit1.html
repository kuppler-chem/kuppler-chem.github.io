Unit Overview

In this 45-day unit, students build the foundations of chemistry: measurement & uncertainty, properties and classification of matter, atomic structure and isotopes, periodic patterns and electron structure, and core lab skills used all year.

Standards for Unit 1:  HS-PS1-1 HS-PS1-2 HS-PS1-8

Essential Questions

  1. EQ1: How do models of atomic structure and periodic patterns help us explain and predict the properties of matter?
  2. EQ2: How do measurement choices (tools, precision, error) impact the quality and reliability of scientific conclusions?

▶ Week 1 (Days 1–5) — Lab Safety, Measurement, Precision

Focus Safety, SI units, significant figures, error types, lab technique.

Day 1 — Course Intro & Safety

Objective: Identify safety symbols, PPE, and classroom lab procedures.

In class: Lab safety contract; safety tour; demo of safe flame use.

Homework: Safety contract + parent signature.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — identify one hazard and mitigation.

Day 2 — Measurement & SI

Objective: Use metric units and estimate with precision.

In class: Stations: mass, volume, length; estimate uncertainty.

Homework: Unit conversions practice.

Checkpoint: Quick check — report measurement with sig figs.

Day 3 — Significant Figures

Objective: Apply sig-fig rules in calculations (×/÷ and +/−).

In class: Mini-lesson; mixed practice; error analysis.

Homework: Sig-fig worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — round correctly.

Day 4 — Density & Error

Objective: Calculate density; distinguish accuracy vs precision; percent error.

In class: Sample density problems; glassware comparison.

Homework: Density practice set.

Checkpoint: Quick check — % error calc.

Day 5 — Lab: Density of Metals

Objective: Determine density via displacement; evaluate precision.

Homework: Finish calculations; discuss sources of error.

Checkpoint: Lab check — table complete with units.

▶ Week 2 (Days 6–10) — States of Matter & Separation

Focus Physical vs chemical changes, mixtures vs pure substances, separation techniques.

Day 6 — Classification of Matter

Objective: Differentiate elements, compounds, homogeneous/heterogeneous mixtures.

In class: Sorting activity; particulate diagrams.

Homework: Vocab & diagrams.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — classify sample cards.

Day 7 — Physical vs Chemical Change

Objective: Identify evidence of chemical change; practice claims with evidence.

In class: Demo set; CER mini-writes.

Homework: Change classification practice.

Checkpoint: Quick check — identify chemical change.

Day 8 — Separation Techniques

Objective: Choose filtration, distillation, chromatography appropriately.

In class: Technique stations; decision tree.

Homework: Short problem set.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — pick technique for scenario.

Day 9 — Lab: Paper Chromatography

Objective: Separate ink dyes; calculate Rf.

Homework: Complete Rf calculations.

Checkpoint: Lab check — Rf with sig figs.

Day 10 — Formative Quiz & Reteach

Objective: Assess classification & separation concepts.

In class: Short quiz; small-group reteach.

Homework: Corrections.

Checkpoint: Formal formative — quiz.

▶ Week 3 (Days 11–15) — Atomic Structure & Isotopes

Focus Subatomic particles, isotopes, average atomic mass; nuclear basics per HS-PS1-8.

Day 11 — Subatomic Particles

Objective: Determine p⁺, n⁰, e⁻ for neutral atoms and ions.

In class: Periodic table reference; ion notation.

Homework: Practice set.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — ion composition.

Day 12 — Isotopes & Average Atomic Mass

Objective: Calculate weighted average atomic mass.

In class: “Bean isotope” model; weighted averages.

Homework: Average mass practice.

Checkpoint: Quick check — compute average.

Day 13 — Nuclear Basics (Qualitative)

Objective: Communicate basic info about radioactivity, fission/fusion (HS-PS1-8).

In class: Short reading + discussion; safety context.

Homework: Note-taking prompts.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one everyday application.

Day 14 — Lab: Flame Tests (Qualitative Spectra)

Objective: Associate flame colors with metal ions; link to electron transitions qualitatively.

Homework: Finish tables; error discussion.

Checkpoint: Lab check — correct ion/color matches.

Day 15 — Review & Quiz

Objective: Check understanding of isotopes & basics.

In class: Review circuit; short quiz.

Homework: Corrections.

Checkpoint: Formal formative — quiz.

▶ Week 4 (Days 16–20) — Electron Structure (Honors Depth, no VSEPR)

Focus Bohr → shells/subshells, configurations (spdf), periodic trends; no VSEPR per scope.

Day 16 — Bohr Model & Spectra

Objective: Relate emission lines to electronic transitions qualitatively.

In class: Spectra images; discussion.

Homework: Short reflection.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — energy vs color.

Day 17 — Electron Configurations (spdf)

In class: Config practice; noble-gas shorthand.

Homework: Config worksheet.

Checkpoint: Quick check — configuration of an ion.

Day 18 — Periodic Trends I

Objective: Explain atomic radius & ionization energy trends.

In class: Trend maps; reasoning with effective nuclear charge.

Homework: Trend problems.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — compare two elements.

Day 19 — Periodic Trends II

Objective: Explain electronegativity & reactivity patterns.

In class: Halogen/alkali examples; data interpretation.

Homework: Practice set.

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — trend prediction.

Day 20 — Lab: Periodic Trends (Inquiry)

Objective: Use data to infer trend relationships.

Homework: Write CER conclusion.

Checkpoint: Lab check — claim supported by data.

▶ Week 7 (Days 31–35) — Cumulative Skills & Applications

▶ Week 9 (Days 41–45) — Presentations & Transition

Presentations; reflection; bridge to Unit 2 stoichiometry.

End-of-Unit Project: “What’s This Stuff?” (Materials ID)
Students use measured properties (density, conductivity, qualitative tests) and error analysis to argue for the identity of unknown materials, justifying conclusions with data and discussing uncertainty. (Aligns to HS-PS1-1, HS-PS1-2.)
