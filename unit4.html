Unit Overview

Develop quantitative and particulate models for equilibrium (K, Q, ICE tables). Apply Le Châtelier’s Principle to predict and justify shifts when conditions change (concentration, pressure/volume, temperature). Emphasis on strategy and constraints.

Standards for Unit 4:  HS-PS1-6 HS-PS1-7 HS-PS1-5 HS-PS1-2

Essential Questions

  1. EQ1: What does dynamic equilibrium mean at the particle level, and how can we predict the direction a disturbed system will shift?
  2. EQ2: How do we change conditions strategically to meet real targets (yield, purity, cost, safety) under constraints?

End-of-Unit Problem-Based Assignment: “On-Target Equilibrium”
Meet a client spec by adjusting conditions to achieve a target outcome (e.g., color/intensity or concentration) under time/cost/safety limits. Defend your strategy with K/Q logic, Le Châtelier reasoning, and data. (Aligns to HS-PS1-6, HS-PS1-7.)
