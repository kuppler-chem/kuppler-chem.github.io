Unit Overview

In this 45-day unit, students develop a particle-level and quantitative model of chemical equilibrium. They determine equilibrium constants (Kc, Kp), reason with the reaction quotient (Q vs. K), and apply Le Châtelier’s Principle to predict and justify shifts when conditions change (concentration, pressure/volume, temperature). Labs emphasize visual and measurable systems (FeSCN2+, CoCl42− / Co(H2O)62+), and the unit culminates in a **problem-based** task where students meet a performance target under realistic constraints by manipulating conditions.

Standards for Unit 4:  HS-PS1-6 HS-PS1-7 HS-PS1-5 HS-PS1-2

Essential Questions

  1. EQ1: What does it mean for a chemical system to be at dynamic equilibrium, and how can we predict the direction a disturbed system will shift?
  2. EQ2: How can we strategically change conditions to achieve desired outcomes (safety, cost, yield, quality) in real processes?

Week 1 (Days 1–5) — What is Dynamic Equilibrium?

Focus Forward/reverse reactions, steady-state ideas, macroscopic constancy vs microscopic motion.

Day 1 — Intro to Equilibrium

Objective: Describe dynamic equilibrium qualitatively and contrast with “stopped” reactions.

In class: PhET demo; particle diagrams for forward/reverse processes.

Homework: Reading notes: equilibrium vocabulary.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — define “dynamic.”

Day 2 — Forward vs Reverse Rates

Objective: Explain equilibrium as equal forward and reverse rates.

In class: Simple kinetic model; graph rate vs time.

Homework: Concept questions.

Checkpoint: Quick check — what is true of rates at equilibrium?

Day 3 — Constant Concentrations

Objective: Interpret concentration vs time graphs at and approaching equilibrium.

In class: Graph analysis; connect to particulate reasoning.

Homework: Sketch approach-to-equilibrium curves.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — identify when equilibrium is reached.

Day 4 — Lab Prep: Visual Equilibria

Objective: Prepare for FeSCN2+ / CoCl42− visual equilibrium labs.

In class: Safety; colorimetry basics; pre-lab questions.

Homework: Pre-lab: predict qualitative shifts.

Checkpoint: Pre-lab check — hazards & PPE identified.

Day 5 — Lab: Visual Equilibrium Survey

Objective: Observe reversible color changes for two systems; note qualitative trends.

In class: Stations: Fe3+ + SCN− ⇌ FeSCN2+ (blood-red); CoCl42− + 6H2O ⇌ Co(H2O)62+ + 4Cl− (blue/pink).

Homework: Complete observations & initial explanations.

Checkpoint: Lab check — observations table complete.

Problem-Based Assignment: “On-Target Equilibrium”
Teams receive a client specification (e.g., achieve a target product concentration/intensity within limits on time, temperature, pressure/volume, and safety). Using Le Châtelier’s Principle, Q vs K reasoning, and quantitative supports (ICE/K where appropriate), students propose and defend an operating strategy (what to change, by how much, and why). Deliverable: a concise solution brief with data, graphs, and justification.
Standards alignment: HS-PS1-6, HS-PS1-7, HS-PS1-5.
