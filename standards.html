Overview

This page lists the NGSS high school Physical Science performance expectations (PEs) addressed in each unit of Honors Chemistry at Winslow Township High School, with links to official NGSS descriptions.

All PEs used this year: HS-PS1-1 HS-PS1-2 HS-PS1-3 HS-PS1-4 HS-PS1-5 HS-PS1-6 HS-PS1-7 HS-PS1-8 HS-PS3-1 HS-PS3-4

Unit 1 — Introduction to Chemistry, Atomic Theory, and Naming Compounds

  • HS-PS1-1: Use the periodic table as a model to predict relative properties of elements based on electron patterns.
  • HS-PS1-2: Construct/revise explanations for outcomes of simple chemical reactions using atomic-level evidence.
  • HS-PS1-3: Plan/conduct investigations to gather evidence for structure–property relationships at the bulk scale.
  • HS-PS1-8: Develop models to illustrate changes in nuclear composition and energy in nuclear processes.

Unit 2 — Chemical Reactions & Stoichiometry

  • HS-PS1-2: Explain/predict reaction outcomes using particle-level reasoning and balanced equations.
  • HS-PS1-3: Investigate how macroscopic properties reflect composition and structure (e.g., solution behavior).
  • HS-PS1-5: Apply principles and evidence to explain how temperature/concentration affect reaction rate (intro focus).
  • HS-PS1-7: Use mathematical representations to support the claim atoms/mass are conserved in reactions (stoichiometry).

Unit 3 — Thermochemistry & Energy

  • HS-PS1-4: Develop a model to illustrate that energy is released or absorbed during chemical reactions (endo/exo).
  • HS-PS3-1: Create computational/quantitative models tracking energy changes among system components.
  • HS-PS3-4: Plan/conduct investigations showing how energy transfer changes thermal energy of components.

Unit 4 — Kinetics, Equilibrium & Le Châtelier’s Principle

  • HS-PS1-5: Explain effects of temperature/concentration on reaction rate using evidence and models (kinetics).
  • HS-PS1-6: Refine designs/conditions to shift equilibrium and change product amounts (Le Châtelier).
