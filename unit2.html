Unit Overview

In this 45-day unit, students learn to represent and quantify chemical change using balanced equations and the mole concept. They classify reaction types, perform stoichiometric calculations (including limiting reactant and percent yield), and apply concepts to gases and solutions. The unit culminates in a project where students optimize a real-world production scenario using stoichiometry.

Standards for Unit 2:  HS-PS1-2 HS-PS1-3 HS-PS1-5 HS-PS1-7

Essential Questions

  1. EQ1: How do balanced chemical equations allow us to quantify and predict the amounts of substances in real reactions?
  2. EQ2: What factors limit real chemical processes, and how can we optimize yield and efficiency?

Week 1 (Days 1–5) — Chemical Equations & Reaction Types

Focus Writing/balancing equations; evidence of chemical change; classifying reactions.

Day 1 — Evidence of Chemical Change

Objective: Identify indicators of chemical reactions (gas, precipitate, temperature, color change).

In class: Demo set (vinegar/baking soda, precipitate formation); claim–evidence–reasoning discussion.

Homework: Reading: reaction evidence; short reflection.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — identify evidence in a new scenario.

Day 2 — Symbols & States in Equations

Objective: Use chemical symbols, formulas, and state symbols correctly.

In class: Notation mini-lesson; practice translating words→formulas.

Homework: Formula/notation practice set.

Checkpoint: Quick check — write states for given equations.

Day 3 — Balancing Chemical Equations (Intro)

Objective: Balance equations using coefficients to satisfy conservation of mass.

In class: Direct instruction + scaffolded practice; conservation demo.

Homework: Balancing worksheet (Level 1).

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — balance 1 equation correctly.

Day 4 — Reaction Types

Objective: Classify reactions (synthesis, decomposition, single replacement, double replacement, combustion).

In class: Pattern matching; identify products in common forms.

Homework: Reaction classification practice.

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — identify reaction types.

Day 5 — Lab: Reaction Types Survey

Objective: Observe & classify reactions; write balanced equations for each.

In class: Station lab (precipitation, single-replacement metal + acid, combustion of a hydrocarbon demo).

Homework: Complete lab write-up with balanced equations.

Checkpoint: Lab check — correct classification + balanced equations.

Week 2 (Days 6–10) — Mole Concept & Ratios

Focus Mole concept, molar mass, percent composition, empirical & molecular formulas.

Day 6 — The Mole & Avogadro’s Number

Objective: Define the mole and relate particles↔moles↔mass.

In class: Conversion ladder; particle analogies.

Homework: Mole conversion practice (particles↔moles↔grams).

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — convert given particles to grams.

Day 7 — Molar Mass & Conversions

Objective: Calculate molar mass and perform two-step conversions.

In class: Periodic table practice; error-analysis problems.

Homework: Mixed conversion set.

Checkpoint: Quick check — molar mass of a hydrate & convert to moles.

Day 8 — Percent Composition & Hydrates

Objective: Determine percent composition and analyze hydrates.

In class: Examples and guided practice; hydrate mass loss problems.

Homework: Percent composition worksheet.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — composition of a binary compound.

Day 9 — Empirical & Molecular Formulas

Objective: Derive empirical and molecular formulas from data.

In class: Combustion analysis problems; percent→moles→ratio.

Homework: Empirical/molecular set.

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — empirical formula from % data.

Day 10 — Formative Quiz + Reteach

Objective: Assess and reinforce mole skills.

In class: Short quiz; targeted reteach rotations.

Homework: Correct errors; optional challenge problems.

Checkpoint: Formal formative — quiz.

Week 3 (Days 11–15) — Stoichiometry Fundamentals

Focus Mole ratios from balanced equations; mass–mass, mass–moles, particles–mass conversions.

Day 11 — Stoichiometric Ratios

Objective: Use coefficients as mole ratios.

In class: Translate balanced equations to ratios; simple mass→mass problems.

Homework: Ratio practice set.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — identify correct mole ratio for a product.

Day 12 — Mass–Mass Problems

Objective: Solve two- and three-step stoichiometry problems.

In class: Worked examples; partner practice; solution checks.

Homework: Mass–mass problem set.

Checkpoint: Quick check — grams reactant → grams product.

Day 13 — Particles & Volume Variations

Objective: Include particles (Avogadro’s number) and gas volume at STP.

In class: Mixed problems; note assumptions at STP.

Homework: Mixed stoichiometry practice.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — moles→L at STP.

Day 14 — Limiting Reactant (Concepts)

Objective: Identify limiting/excess reactants with models and numeric data.

In class: PhET “Reactants, Products & Leftovers”; pizza/recipe analogies → balanced equations.

Homework: Limiting reactant practice set (intro).

Checkpoint: Quick quiz — identify LR from given quantities.

Day 15 — Percent Yield (Concepts)

Objective: Distinguish theoretical vs. actual yield; compute percent yield.

In class: Example walkthroughs; common sources of loss.

Homework: Percent yield problems.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — calculate percent yield from data.

Week 4 (Days 16–20) — Labs: Limiting Reactant & Percent Yield

Focus Hands-on limiting reactant (parallel reactions by groups) and a percent yield synthesis.

Day 16 — Lab Setup & Safety Briefing

Objective: Prepare for Limiting Reactant Lab; review procedures & safety.

In class: Pre-lab questions; station assignments; calibration of balances.

Homework: Pre-lab calculations (predict LR for assigned reaction).

Checkpoint: Pre-lab check — predicted LR correct.

Day 17 — Lab: Limiting Reactant (Group Variations)

Objective: Determine the limiting reactant empirically using different reaction pairs.

In class: Parallel reactions by groups (e.g., precipitation: Na₂CO₃ + CaCl₂ → CaCO₃↓; gas-forming: NaHCO₃ + HCl → CO₂↑; metal displacement: CuSO₄ + Fe → Cu).

Homework: Begin calculations; identify LR and theoretical yield.

Checkpoint: Lab check — data table complete.

Day 18 — Lab Workup: Limiting Reactant

Objective: Calculate theoretical, actual, and percent yield; analyze error.

In class: Finish calculations; error analysis discussion across groups.

Homework: Formal lab report draft.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — defend LR choice with data.

Day 19 — Lab: Percent Yield (Copper Cycle or Synthesis)

Objective: Perform a reaction to determine percent yield from collected product.

In class: Example: Synthesize Cu from CuSO₄ + Fe; filter, dry, weigh.

Homework: Compute yields and sources of error.

Checkpoint: Lab check — theoretical vs actual yield computed.

Day 20 — Lab Debrief & Reteach

Objective: Compare results; connect to EQs (limits & optimization).

In class: Cross-group presentations; common pitfalls; tie to industry scale-up.

Homework: Lab report revisions.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — one way to improve yield.

Week 5 (Days 21–25) — Solution Stoichiometry & Intro Titrations

Focus Molarity, dilution, solution stoichiometry, introduction to titration.

Day 21 — Molarity & Dilution

Objective: Calculate molarity; perform dilution problems (M₁V₁ = M₂V₂).

In class: Practice sets; making a standard solution demo.

Homework: Molarity/dilution problems.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — compute volume needed for a target M.

Day 22 — Solution Stoichiometry

Objective: Use molarity in stoichiometric calculations (vol→mol→mol→vol/mass).

In class: Guided problems; precipitation context.

Homework: Solution stoichiometry set.

Checkpoint: Quick check — volume of product formed.

Day 23 — Intro to Titrations

Objective: Understand titration setup and endpoint concepts.

In class: Virtual titration (ChemCollective) + sample calculation.

Homework: Pre-lab titration questions.

Checkpoint: Exit ticket — identify endpoint vs equivalence.

Day 24 — Lab: Acid–Base Titration (Intro)

Objective: Determine concentration via titration data.

In class: Run simple monoprotic acid–base titration; capture volumes; compute M.

Homework: Finish calculations; error sources.

Checkpoint: Lab check — concentration reported with sig figs.

Day 25 — Titration Workup & Quiz

Objective: Finalize titration analysis; assess solution stoich skills.

In class: Debrief; short quiz (molarity, dilution, solution stoich).

Homework: Corrections or extension tasks.

Checkpoint: Formal formative — quiz.

Day 28 — Lab: Gas Collection

Homework: Calculate theoretical vs collected volume; % error.

Checkpoint: Lab check — volume analysis complete.

End-of-Unit Project: “Stoichiometry in Industry”
Teams design and justify a small-scale production plan (e.g., neutralizing acid waste, producing a set mass of product, or compounding a fertilizer). They must show balanced equations, limiting reactant analysis, materials and cost estimates, predicted vs. target yield, and optimizations to minimize waste.
Standards alignment: HS-PS1-7, HS-PS1-3, HS-PS1-5.
